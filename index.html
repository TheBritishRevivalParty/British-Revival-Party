The rapid development of AI

April 27, 2023

ChatGPT has taken the world by storm with its in-depth and often human answers to our questions ranging from ideas for a child’s birthday party all the way up to quantum physics.

While it is undeniable ChatGPT has amazing applications for the real world, many people are against it; Italy for example recently banned it as they were concerned about its privacy policy, this ban was later reversed however.

Our response

Many others, including those in the UK government, have strong and misguided opinions about advanced artificial intelligences such as ChatGPT. Public perception of AI has been shaped by fears of them revolting and overtaking our society. This has not been helped by massively popular franchises (eg. James Cameron's Terminator, which features malevolent AIs at the core of their story). The concept of something better than us, something smarter is intrinsically horrifying. A common worry amongst the public is that advanced AIs such as ChatGPT will take over their jobs. We at the BRP don’t think this will happen; AIs right now, while powerful, are severely limited. Many of them lack critical thinking skills and the ability to innovate, furthermore, we want to protect your rights as a citizen of this country.

We believe strongly in the philosophy of equal opportunities for everyone and that extends to jobs and we want to incorporate AIs into the workplace as they are invaluable tools. For instance, they are skilled at categorising large sums of data, this frees up workers to work on things AIs cannot, improving efficiency in the workplace. Another concern regarding ChatGPT is it’s often biased and can produce false answers, as we know the internet is a breeding ground for misinformation and this carries onto ChatGPT. ChatGPT’s safety filters are fairly easy to bypass with the correct wording, this has become synonymous with the “DAN exploit” wherein you trick the AI into roleplaying as an AI with no morals. We would hope to limit this by training AIs on very specific sets of data related to the function we want them to do. This would have the effect of making them more efficient while simultaneously making them safer for those using them.

Productivity in the UK has stayed low since the financial crisis, and mainstream adoption of AI in the workplace could be the paradigm shift needed to get the UK back into a phase of high economic growth and opportunity. If AI can work with people rather than instead of them, our businesses, people, and our country as a whole could thrive.
